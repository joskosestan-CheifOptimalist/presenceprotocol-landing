Presence Protocol

Proof of Presence for the real world.

Presence Protocol explores privacy-conscious cryptographic verification of meaningful human co-presence. After Proof of Work and Proof of Stake, it proposes Proof of Presence as a new coordination primitive for real-world participation without defaulting to surveillance, biometric dependency, or centralized identity.

Protocol stack

Four coordinated layers, one constitutional-grade verification thesis.

Presence Protocol is not simply a mining app or token concept. It is a modular infrastructure thesis exploring whether physical co-presence can become a verifiable digital event while preserving privacy, consent, and human dignity.

Presence Engine

Deterministic proximity handshakes convert mutual physical presence into signed encounter events.

Encounter Validation

Dual signatures, cooldown rules, and anomaly checks verify that participation is real and not farmed.

Relay Network

Distributed relays propagate encounter receipts for offline-first synchronization and verification.

Settlement & Privacy

Cardano provides transparent settlement while Midnight protects privacy-sensitive participation data.

Participation lifecycle

Deterministic Proof-of-Presence flow.

Scan

Devices discover nearby participants through energy-efficient proximity scanning.

Handshake

Ephemeral keys and dual signatures confirm mutual presence without relying on GPS identity databases.

Queue

Encounter receipts are stored locally so participation remains viable even when devices are offline.

Verify

Relay infrastructure enforces protocol rules, signature validity, cooldown windows, and fraud checks.

Settle

Accepted encounters produce a verifiable participation record eligible for protocol rewards and future applications.

Constitutional principles

Designed to prove enough without revealing too much.

Consent First

Participation should remain voluntary and understandable.

Privacy by Design

Presence proofs should minimize exposed personal data.

Minimal Disclosure

The protocol should reveal only what is necessary for verification.

Open Verification

Protocol rules, assumptions, and limits should be auditable.

Human-Centred Infrastructure

The system exists to support coordination, not behavioural surveillance.

No Central Identity Dependency

The base protocol should not require mandatory biometric or state identity infrastructure.

Threat model
