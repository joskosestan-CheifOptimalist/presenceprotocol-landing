Presence Protocol Whitepaper

Proof of Presence

Presence Protocol explores whether meaningful real-world human co-presence can become a privacy-conscious cryptographic primitive for decentralized systems.

After Proof of Work and Proof of Stake, Presence Protocol proposes Proof of Presence as a new coordination layer for verifiable participation without defaulting to surveillance, centralized identity infrastructure, or biometric dependency.

1. The Problem

Modern digital systems are highly effective at verifying transactions, signatures, and ownership, yet remain weak at verifying meaningful real-world participation.

Current approaches frequently rely on:

  • centralized identity providers
  • platform-controlled verification
  • GPS tracking
  • biometric dependency
  • behavioral surveillance

Presence Protocol explores whether cryptographic co-presence proofs can provide an alternative coordination primitive for decentralized systems.

2. Proof of Presence

Proof of Presence is the proposed cryptographic verification of meaningful co-presence events between consenting participants.

Using local device proximity, ephemeral keys, deterministic encounter validation, and relay verification, Presence Protocol attempts to establish verifiable participation while minimizing unnecessary disclosure of identity and movement data.

3. Constitutional Principles

Consent First

Participation should remain voluntary and understandable.

Privacy by Design

The protocol should prove enough without revealing too much.

Minimal Disclosure

Presence proofs should minimize exposed personal data.

Open Verification

Protocol assumptions and rules should remain auditable.

Human-Centred Infrastructure

The protocol exists to support coordination, not behavioral surveillance.

No Mandatory Identity Layer

The protocol should not require centralized biometric or state identity systems.

4. Threat Model & Safeguards

Potential Risks

  • coercive attendance systems
  • employer misuse
  • state overreach
  • social graph extraction
  • replay attacks
  • sybil manipulation
  • location deanonymization
  • fabricated encounter environments

Research Directions

  • selective disclosure
  • privacy-preserving verification
  • local-first verification
  • cryptographic minimization
  • relay anomaly detection
  • Midnight-aligned privacy architecture
  • constitutional governance review

5. Governance Alignment

Presence Protocol aligns with Cardano’s long-term direction toward governance, public-good infrastructure, and privacy-conscious decentralized coordination.

Rather than functioning purely as a speculative token system, Presence Protocol is being developed as a research-driven infrastructure thesis exploring how decentralized systems may verify meaningful participation while preserving human dignity and civil liberties.

Future research may explore compatibility with:

  • Cardano governance frameworks
  • Midnight privacy infrastructure
  • decentralized attestations
  • public-good coordination systems
  • privacy-preserving participation records

6. Limitations

  • Proof of Presence does not prove intent.
  • Proof of Presence does not replace human trust.
  • Proof of Presence should not become compulsory infrastructure.
  • Presence verification alone cannot solve governance legitimacy.
  • The protocol remains experimental and under active research.

7. Current Development Status

  • mobile MVP experimentation
  • protocol-layer research and documentation
